Introduction

India is an emerging economy It is already an IT hub and is striving to become the manufacturing hub of the world as well Besides it is also rapidly improving its infrastructure by building up a lot of new roads bridges hospitals etc All these activities are making India an energy hungry nation For long the country has relied on Conventional Sources of energy but those sources are drying up drying up fast To address all these issues India needs to nurture a new breed of engineers technologists who would be able to develop new technologies to reduce dependency on Conventional Sources of Energy utilize the vast reserve of Non Conventional Sources which have not yet been successfully tapped in plenty To nurture the engineers of tomorrow the teachers of today need to be fully aware of such activities that are happening all around the world Hence a Faculty Development Programme FDP has to be conducted to acquaint the teachers with the latest in the field of energy so that they can carry the same to the students The knowledge thus transferred will render the world of education richer and wiser

About the programme

Energy is the buzzword today There is an ever increasing demand of energy throughout the world and more so in India In the North Eastern part of India too energy crisis has driven the government to announce new schemes to encourage investments in this sector There is a tremendous technological advancement worldwide in the energy Latest technology in energy and power sector can mitigate the global issue of the energy crisis This FDP will be useful to faculties of engineering colleges and young scientists from other academic institutes The FDP will try to bridge the gap between the latest developments in the energy sector industry and the academia of the North East region With few senior faculties from IITs NITs Central Universities and representatives from the govt sector being the resource persons the participants will gain an understanding of the emerging trends and the challenges faced by the industry in this sector The various public sector undertakings in the North East will give an overview of the energy scenario in the region including the renewable energy The faculties can then discuss these issues with the students and improve the teaching learning process They can motivate the students to take up projects in this field

Programme Outcomes

With an aim to meet the never ending energy demands of the current world in an eco friendly manner this Faculty Development Programme will provide a forum for exchange of views ideas the latest innovations in the field of energy between the industrial and academic sectors It will also be a platform for the participants to learn about the basics emerging trends challenges in the field of renewable energy from some of the experts of our country in this field and expose the faculty members to the concepts of Recent Advances in energy technology with special reference to North East India The field visit will expose the participants to the practical aspects of the Energy Sector We believe that this programme designed to promote the professionals in the area of energy sector will prepare them to act as Resource Persons in guiding and motivating young students towards creating a greener and cleaner world It will allow our next generation of energy decision makers to assess what is actually happening in the world now and gauge what will happen in the future including the impact of today s choices on tomorrow s energy landscape

Who Can Attend

The course is open to all the faculties of engineering colleges, polytechnics and scienec colleges, universities approved by UGC/AICTE. Maximum number of participants in the programme is 32 and selection is based on first come first serve basis. Attendance in all session is mandatory to receive the participation certificate.
